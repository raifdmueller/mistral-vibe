= Open Questions — Mistral Vibe Theory Recovery
:toc: left
:sectnums:

This is the handoff document of Phase 1. Each question below could not be
answered from source code. Questions are routed by the role best placed to
answer them. The team answers these; Phase 2 then synthesizes documentation
from the completed tree. Every question keeps its Q-ID for traceability.

NOTE: For this experiment run no team is available. Phase 2 documents therefore
mark each dependent claim `[OPEN — see Q-X.Y]` rather than inventing answers,
and ADRs carry Status "Accepted (inferred)".

== Product Owner

* *Q1.2.1* — Are there distinct user personas beyond a generic "developer"
(for example: solo open-source maintainer, enterprise team member, CI
operator)? What are their goals and skill levels?
* *Q1.4* — Why was Mistral Vibe built? What is the strategic motivation and
market position relative to other CLI coding assistants?
* *Q1.5* — Which metric defines product success (adoption, retention,
task-completion rate, conversion to the cloud product)?
* *Q1.6* — What is the priority order across developer segments
(individual vs. team vs. CI/automation)?
* *Q4.9* — Across the ISO 25010 characteristics, which one has top priority
when trade-offs are forced? (Code signals suggest Maintainability and Security.)

== Architect

* *Q3.6* — Confirm the runtime ordering for the ACP-session and teleport
sequences, especially error-path and cancellation edges.
* *Q3.9* — For each inferred ADR, confirm the rationale and the alternatives
that were actually rejected.
* *Q3.10* — What are the measurable quality scenarios and targets
(startup-time SLA, per-turn latency budget, memory ceiling)?
* *Q4.2* — Is there a stated startup-time or turn-latency budget?
* *Q4.9* — (shared with Product Owner) Confirm the quality-goal ranking.
* *Q5.5* — What are the known performance ceilings on very large repositories
(file-indexing cost, context size)?

== Developer

codes.
* *Q2.5* — Author Gherkin acceptance criteria for the use cases and trace each
to its UC and BR IDs; the existing 228 test files are not written this way.
* *Q2.6* — Confirm the consolidated Business-Rule register and assign stable
BR-IDs beyond BR-013/BR-014.
